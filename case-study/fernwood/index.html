01 / Overview

A working dental site that needed local keyword clarity.

Fernwood Dental Care is a family dentist practice in South Austin, Texas. The site was already functional and loading fast — the SEO gaps were about local relevance signals, not technical fundamentals.

The H1 read "Gentle dentistry, done right." — a tagline, not a search-intent statement. The title, headings, and meta description didn't include "South Austin" or "family dentistry." No sitemap was present. Schema markup existed but was missing key fields for a healthcare business. Keywords were scattered rather than consistent across title, meta, and headings.

02 / What changed

Six targeted changes, measurable audit lift.

01

H1 rewritten with local intent

"Gentle dentistry, done right." became "Gentle family dentistry in South Austin." — connecting the primary keyword to location in the most prominent on-page heading.

On-Page · Local SEO
02

Title and meta aligned to local search

Title tag and meta description were rewritten to consistently include "South Austin" and "family dentistry," matching the search terms a patient would actually type.

On-Page
03

Sitemap added and submitted

No sitemap existed before. A sitemap.xml covering all 18 pages was created and referenced in robots.txt, making the crawl path explicit for search engines.

Technical SEO
04

Schema markup enhanced

The existing LocalBusiness schema was extended with medicalSpecialty, currenciesAccepted, and paymentAccepted fields — standard signals for a healthcare practice.

Schema
05

llms.txt added for AI search

A /llms.txt file was added describing the site's content and purpose — an emerging best practice that pushed SEOptimer's GEO score from C+ to A+.

GEO · AI Search
06

Email removed from plaintext, external link added

The email address was replaced with a contact form reference to avoid spam scraping. An outbound link to MouthHealthy.org (ADA) added a relevant external authority signal.

On-Page · Trust

04 / Limitations

What these scores do not prove.

01

No booking or ranking data

The scores reflect on-page and technical signals audited by tools. They do not confirm any change in appointment volume, Google rankings, or map-pack visibility.

02

Semrush crawl is time-delayed

Semrush shows the same 87%/88% for both runs because site crawls reflect previously indexed state. The unchanged score is expected, not a sign that the changes were ineffective.

03

External authority is still weak

Seobility still reports external factors at 3%. One outbound link to MouthHealthy.org was added; inbound backlinks were not part of this engagement.

04

Links score remains F in SEOptimer

SEOptimer consistently grades links as F for new or small local sites without an established backlink profile. This is a known limitation of local business SEO at this stage.
